# Sample Output — Competitive Outbound Sequence

> **Query:** `Turn the Salesforce signal into a 6-email outbound sequence for prospects evaluating or already on Salesforce.`
> **Generated by:** the same indexed G2 review set, re-prompted for a sequence instead of a battle card — same retrieval layer, different output contract.
> Target persona: RevOps / Sales leader at a mid-market company. Quotes are representative redacted examples of real G2 review signal.

---

## Sequence at a glance

| # | Send timing | Angle | Primary CTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | Cost unpredictability | "Worth a look?" |
| 2 | Day 3 | Adoption / rep workarounds | Soft question |
| 3 | Day 6 | Time-to-value | Resource share |
| 4 | Day 9 | Social proof (switcher story) | Case study |
| 5 | Day 13 | Direct ask | Book 20 min |
| 6 | Day 18 | Breakup | Permission to close |

## Email 1 — Day 0 · The cost angle

**Subject line options**
- A: `your Salesforce renewal math`
- B: `the part of Salesforce nobody budgets for`
- C: `quick question on your CRM spend`

**Body**

Hi {{first_name}},

Most teams I talk to didn't pick Salesforce because of the license cost — they picked it because it's the default. The surprise shows up later: the admin headcount, the consultants, and the add-ons that turn a CRM line item into a cost center.

One RevOps lead put it bluntly in a public review: *"the cost kept climbing every renewal and we couldn't predict it."*

If forecasting your CRM spend has gotten harder, might be worth a look at how {{company}} handles it differently.

Worth a quick comparison?

{{sender_name}}

**Objection pre-empt:** opens on *unpredictable cost*, not "Salesforce is bad" — keeps it about their budget, not a vendor fight.

## Email 2 — Day 3 · The adoption angle

**Subject line options**
- A: `are your reps actually using it?`
- B: `re: pipeline data you can trust`
- C: `the spreadsheet problem`

**Body**

Hi {{first_name}},

Quick follow-up with a question instead of a pitch: how clean is your pipeline data right now?

The pattern I keep seeing in Salesforce reviews is reps quietly routing around the system — *"logging a call takes too many clicks, so reps just stopped doing it."* When that happens, you're paying enterprise prices for spreadsheet-grade data, and every forecast inherits the gap.

If that rings true even a little, happy to show you what changes when logging activity takes one click instead of six.

{{sender_name}}

**Objection handler:** if they say *"our adoption is fine"* → "Great — then this is a 10-minute confirmation, not a sales call. What % of closed-won has complete activity history today?"

## Email 4 — Day 9 · The switcher story

**Subject line options**
- A: `how {{similar_company}} got off the renewal treadmill`
- B: `a team that made the switch`
- C: `proof, not pitch`

**Body**

Hi {{first_name}},

Rather than make the case myself, here's a team that already did it: a mid-market org that left Salesforce after its renewal became impossible to forecast and rep adoption stalled.

Short version: predictable pricing, one team live in under a month, activity logging that reps actually use. Full story here → [case study link]

## Email 5 — Day 13 · The direct ask

**Subject line options**
- A: `20 minutes?`
- B: `worth a side-by-side?`
- C: `{{company}} + a real cost comparison`

**Body**

Hi {{first_name}},

I'll be direct: I think there's a real side-by-side worth running for {{company}} — your current Salesforce spend (license + admin + consultants + add-ons) against a setup with predictable pricing and faster adoption.

Twenty minutes, screen share, I'll bring the framework. Does Thursday or Friday work?

{{sender_name}}

**Objection handler:** if they say *"send me pricing first"* → "Will do — but pricing only means something against your real TCO. The 20 minutes is how we get an apples-to-apples number worth comparing."

## Email 6 — Day 18 · The breakup

**Subject line options**
- A: `closing the loop`
- B: `should I stop here?`
- C: `last one from me`

**Body**

Hi {{first_name}},

I've reached out a few times on the cost and adoption side of Salesforce — I don't want to keep landing in your inbox if the timing's wrong.

If it's not a priority this quarter, just reply "not now" and I'll circle back later. If forecastable CRM spend and actual rep adoption *are* on your list, I'm one reply away.

Either way, thanks for the read.

{{sender_name}}

**Why the breakup works:** gives an easy out, which often surfaces the real "not now / wrong person / send it Q3" — better than silence, and it keeps the door open.
