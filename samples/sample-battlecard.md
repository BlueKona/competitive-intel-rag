# Sample Output — Battle Card

> **Query:** `Why do people leave Salesforce?`
> **Generated by:** the Competitor Intelligence Chat workflow, retrieving from the indexed G2 review set.
> Quotes below are representative examples of the kind of verbatim signal the pipeline surfaces from public G2 reviews. Names and identifying details are redacted.

---

🚨 **WHY CUSTOMERS LEAVE SALESFORCE**

- **Total cost of ownership balloons past the sticker price.** The recurring theme isn't the license — it's the admin headcount, the consultants, and the per-feature add-ons. Reviewers describe budgeting for a CRM and ending up with a cost center.
- **Time-to-value is measured in quarters, not weeks.** Implementation and customization are repeatedly called slow and dependent on specialized (often external) expertise. Smaller teams report stalling out before they ever get clean adoption.
- **Day-to-day UI friction taxes the reps.** Reviewers cite a cluttered interface, too many clicks to log activity, and reps quietly working around the system — which degrades the data the whole org depends on.
- **Support quality doesn't match the price tier.** Slow resolution and premium-priced support tiers come up often, especially from mid-market accounts who feel under-served relative to enterprise logos.

📊 **SIGNAL STRENGTH**
Cost and complexity were the two most frequently recurring themes across the retrieved reviews; support and UI friction appeared as strong secondary patterns. (This is directional signal from public reviews, not a statistically weighted sample — see the README's "what I'd do differently" note on eval.)

💬 **EXACT QUOTES FOR COLD OUTREACH**

> "The platform is powerful but you basically need a full-time admin (or a paid consultant) to make it do anything useful."

> "We switched because the cost kept climbing every renewal and we couldn't predict it. Every new feature was another line item."

> "Onboarding took months. By the time we were live, half the team had gone back to spreadsheets."

> "Logging a call takes too many clicks. Reps just stopped doing it, so our pipeline data was never trustworthy."

---

🛡️ **OBJECTION HANDLER**

| When the prospect says… | Counter move |
|---|---|
| "We're already invested in Salesforce." | "Most teams we talk to aren't leaving the data — they're leaving the overhead. What does your current admin + consultant spend look like annually? That's usually where the real comparison is." |
| "It's the industry standard, nobody gets fired for buying it." | "Agreed it's the default. The question is adoption — if reps are working around it, you're paying enterprise prices for spreadsheet-grade data. How clean is your pipeline data actually?" |
| "Switching costs are too high." | "Fair. What's the cost of another year of low adoption and a renewal you can't forecast? We can scope a parallel pilot on one team so nobody bets the org on day one." |

---

📌 **PITCH ANGLE IN ONE LINE**

*"Salesforce sells you the platform; the bill is the admins, consultants, and add-ons it takes to run it — we get one team live and adopting in weeks, with a price you can actually forecast."*
